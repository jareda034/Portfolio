Project Overview

Game Summary: Cyber Threat is a 3D top-down shooter built in Unity featuring enemy AI, tactical shooting mechanics, mission objectives, and interactive gameplay systems. Players battle hostile enemies while completing objectives and progressing through increasingly dangerous combat scenarios.

Core Goal: The project was designed to combine engaging third-person combat with intelligent enemy behaviours and mission-based gameplay. Players must strategically eliminate enemies, manage ammunition, and complete objectives to progress.

Development Context: Cyber Threat was developed as a solo Unity project to improve my understanding of AI behaviour, NavMesh navigation, combat systems, mission design, and object-oriented gameplay architecture in a 3D environment.

What I Learned: Throughout development I gained practical experience building state-based enemy AI, combat systems, shooting mechanics, mission frameworks, interaction systems, event-driven gameplay, level design, and debugging complex gameplay interactions.

Biggest Challenge: One of the biggest challenges was developing enemy AI that could smoothly transition between patrol, detection, chase, and attack states while maintaining reliable navigation and responsive combat behaviour.

Gameplay Features

Combat and Enemy AI

Combat is driven by intelligent enemy AI that patrols designated areas, detects the player, pursues them using Unity's NavMesh system, and attacks once within range. Enemies also react dynamically when damaged, immediately becoming aggressive and chasing the player.

Implemented Features

  • Enemy patrol system.
  • Player detection and chase AI.
  • State-based AI behaviours.
  • Reactive enemy aggression.
  • Combat and shooting mechanics.
  • Health and damage system.

Mission And Gameplay Systems

Players progress by completing mission objectives while fighting through enemy-controlled areas. Interactive objects and contextual UI prompts guide players through objectives and create a structured gameplay experience.

Gameplay Systems

Weapons And Technical Systems

Players engage enemies using ranged combat supported by weapon reloading, health management, and responsive gameplay systems. The project uses modular, object-oriented code to allow gameplay systems to remain reusable and expandable.

Technical Notes

Cyber Threat was built in Unity using C# and follows an object-oriented, modular architecture. Enemy behaviours are implemented using state-based AI supported by Unity's NavMesh pathfinding system, while gameplay systems communicate through event-driven logic to create responsive combat and mission progression.
