About Me

I am a game developer and programmer who enjoys building interactive experiences that people genuinely connect with.

My interest in games began when I was young. After I was gifted a PlayStation 1, I spent countless hours exploring digital worlds. One of my favorite PS1 games was 3DO Army Men. Later, I got a PlayStation 2 and discovered an even bigger library of titles, including Metal Gear Solid, Ace Combat 4, Call of Duty: World at War, Battlefield 2, Company of Heroes, Gran Turismo 3, and one of my personal favorites, Secret Weapons Over Normandy.

I also played games on my laptop, and one game I played all the time was Goodgame Empire. That was my first experience with management and strategy gameplay. My brother had an Xbox 360, and I used to watch him play The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim. When I finally played it myself, I quickly became obsessed with its world and the freedom it gave players to explore.

Another very important game for me was Disney Infinity, especially Toy Box mode. I spent hours building mini-games with its logic tools, and that is where my interest in creating games really started to grow. Games are more than entertainment to me. They are art, a way to connect with people, a way to tell stories, and a way to discover new things about ourselves.

My goal is to create games that inspire joy and stay with players for years, just like the games from my childhood stayed with me. In 2024, I started a coding course to build my programming foundations. After finishing it, I downloaded Unity and began learning game development and C#. Since then, I have been building projects independently, improving every day, and enjoying the journey.

This portfolio showcases projects I have worked on, including games and software applications.

In each project, I focus on a specific gameplay mechanic or software challenge to push myself and learn new skills. I hope you enjoy exploring my work.
